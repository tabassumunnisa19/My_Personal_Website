About Me

I hold a B.Tech in Electronics & Communication and a Master’s in Digital Communication, which laid the foundation for my professional journey. Over the years, I gained valuable experience in teaching, project coordination, and HR—each role sharpening my ability to analyze, communicate, and adapt to challenges.

Beyond the technical side, I’m passionate about writing, teaching, storytelling, and creativity, often channeling my thoughts and experiences into words that inspire and connect. After taking a career break for family, I am now returning with renewed focus and determination.

I chose data science not because it is an emerging field, but because I discovered it to be the perfect intersection of my skills, experiences, and passion. Through self-reflection, I realized that working with data, solving problems, and creating impact is what excites me the most. I truly love data science—because doing what you love is not only fulfilling, it’s also where you create your best work. For me, data science is not just a career choice; it is a path that feels naturally aligned with who I am and what I genuinely enjoy.

Research Publications

Designing a Dynamic Pricing Engine for E-Commerce: Revenue Optimization and Machine Learning Applications

This research explores the implementation of a dynamic pricing system similar to those used by Amazon, Uber, and major airlines. It combines regression, reinforcement learning, and API-based deployment to optimize pricing and revenue. Using real-world e-commerce datasets, the paper demonstrates modeling market dynamics, predicting demand, and simulating pricing strategies for thousands of products.

Comparative Study of Machine Learning Algorithms on Real-World Datasets

This paper investigates and compares the performance of various supervised and unsupervised machine learning algorithms on diverse real-world datasets, highlighting strengths, weaknesses, and best-use scenarios for each method in practical applications.

A Comparative Survey of Python and R for Data Science: Ecosystems, Applications, and Future Directions

This paper provides a comprehensive comparison of Python and R for data science applications. It analyzes library ecosystems, visualization tools, statistical and machine learning capabilities, and suitability across different domains. The study also highlights practical examples and performance benchmarks, helping beginners and professionals choose the right tool for specific data science tasks.
